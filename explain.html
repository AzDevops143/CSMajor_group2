Underlying Mathematical Model

The SNOOPFINGER attack works by comparing the relative geometry of consecutive orientations (distances and angles) observed in the victim's gaze points to the candidate word geometry of standard dictionary words.

The keyboard consists of standard key coordinates represented as:

\[ K_c = (x_c, y_c) \]

For a typed word \(w\), the victim's raw key center points are perturbed by Gaussian noise representing measurement uncertainty:

\[ P_i = K_{w_i} + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2) \]

Under the coordinate quantization countermeasure of step size \(q\), the coordinates are rounded:

\[ P'_i = \text{round}\left(\frac{P_i}{q}\right) \times q \]

The observed relative geometry is extracted from consecutive points:

\[ \vec{v}_i = P_{i+1} - P_i \implies d_i = \|\vec{v}_i\|_2, \quad \theta_i = \text{arctan2}(\Delta y_i, \Delta x_i) \]

The attacker scores each same-length candidate word \(w_{cand}\) by computing the sum of squared deviations in consecutive distances and circularly wrapped angles:

\[ S(w_{cand}) = \sum_{i=1}^{L} \left( (d_{cand, i} - d_{obs, i})^2 + \left(\frac{\Delta\theta_i}{180}\right)^2 \right) \]

Where \(\Delta\theta_i\) is the circular difference wrapping bounds:

\[ \Delta\theta_i = (\theta_{cand, i} - \theta_{obs, i} + 180)^\circ \pmod{360} - 180^\circ \]
